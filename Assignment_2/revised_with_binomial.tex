\section{Problem}
In a box containing 100 bulbs, 10 are defective. The probability that out of a sample of 5 bulbs, none is defective is
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $10^{-1}$
    \item $\brak{\frac{1}{2}}^5$
    \item $\brak{\frac{9}{10}}^5$
    \item $\frac{9}{10}$
\end{enumerate}
\section{Solution}
Total number of bulbs = 100\\
Number of defective bulbs = 10\\
Probability of a bulb being defective = probability of success = p $=\frac{10}{100} = \frac{1}{10}$\\
Probability of a bulb not being defective = probability of failure = q = 1-p\\$=1- \frac{1}{10} = \frac{9}{10}$\\
Let X be the random variable denoting the number of defective bulbs in a sample of 5 bulbs.\\
Then, $X \sim  Bin(n=5, p)$ \\
$\therefore$ Out of a sample of 5 bulbs, the probability that none are defective is 
\begin{align}
    P(X=0) &= {n \choose x} p^x q^{n-x}\\
    &= {5 \choose 0} \brak{\frac{1}{10}}^{0} \brak{\frac{9}{10}} ^ {5-0}\\
    &= \brak{\frac{9}{10}}^5
\end{align}
Hence, Option \brak 3 is the required answer.
